I've been thinking a lot about what it means to build in public.

There's something powerful about sharing your work as it evolves — the messy parts, the mistakes, the discoveries. It's not just about the final product; it's about the journey.

When I started Milk Crate, I didn't know what I was building. I just knew I wanted to explore how agents could help me understand code better. The project has evolved in ways I didn't expect, and writing about that process has become almost as important as the code itself.

I think there's a parallel to music here. When I'm working on a Body Clock track, the early stages are all about experimentation — trying sounds, making mistakes, following intuition. The finished track is just a snapshot of that process.

Maybe that's what this blog is for: snapshots of the process. Not polished essays, but honest reflections on what I'm learning and building.

We'll see.
